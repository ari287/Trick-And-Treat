Quick tips library

Fast, real-world help between sessions.

Short, saveable tips you can start using today. As we go, this will grow into a library of mini videos and how-tos for everyday life with your dog.

Loose-lead walking

Reward the slack, not the pull.

Any time the lead softens, mark with “yes” and reward by your leg. If they pull again, calmly change direction and give them another chance to find the slack.

Great for daily walks
Puppy focus

Say the name once, then pay attention hard.

Wait until your pup isn't looking at you, say their name once, and reward the instant they flick their eyes towards you ‚Äì with food, pats or play.

Builds reliable name recognition
Calm at the door

Door only opens for four paws on the floor.

If your dog jumps, the door pauses. When they land and pause, mark “yes”, reward, and then the door opens. Calm behaviour makes good things happen.

Helps with guests & deliveries
Reactivity

Think “see dog, eat chicken, move away”.

Spot other dogs early, feed a few tiny treats while you calmly create distance, then let your dog decompress. Recovery speed matters more than silence.

Perfect for “big feelings” dogs
Independence

Start with tiny, boring separations.

Toss a treat on a mat, step into another room for a few seconds, then come back like it's no big deal. Repeat until your dog shrugs you leaving.

Builds confidence at home
Enrichment

Use some meals for “brain work”.

Scatter-feed in the yard, use a cardboard box or simple puzzle toys so your dog can sniff, shred and solve. Tired brains, not just tired legs.

Great on wet & busy days
